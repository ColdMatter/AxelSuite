Axel-data is collection of data processing utilities for different purposes within our cold atom accelerometer experiment. Axel-data is open source (free for any use without any warranty). It is written in C# with use of some NI libraries (Measurement Studio 2019). The source code is HERE.
Enjoy !

Axel Data Processing Desk (Axel-data) 

 
Quantum vs MEMS utility

This data-processing utility is design to accommodate simultaneous quantum interferometer signal with MEMS signal measuring vibrations in the same direction.

When these two signals are plotted against each other a fringe pattern should appear. After fitting a cosine function to it, the parameters of the fit should give us the average acceleration during the measurement period (minutes). The difference of that average estimated acceleration to the average MEMS acceleration  (measurement for the same period of time) can be used as compensation of MEMS drift.

After loading the log file of the measurement, the data are processed by sections (segments). You can process the data section by section with Section Scroll buttons and Fit Single buttons OR you can Run Scan, which will take sections in series and show the fit and the the trend bellow.

The utility provides a simulation mode (with noise) for training and testing as well.
 
Temperature compensation utility

It is well know the the major instability (drift) of MEMS accelerometer is due to its dependence from temperature. This utility provides a tool to estimate a correction (compensation) of that drift by fitting a polynomial of different degree in trying to minimize the fluctuations of measurements of immobile MEMS.

After long enough measurements (days) the coefficients of the polynomial can be a good estimation improving the MEMS accuracy with one or two order of magnitude (x10 - x100).

The residuals and histogram of the residuals are plotted for better illustration of the fit.

 
Join Delay Optimization utility

The measurements of the quantum interferometer signal should match (in time) the measurements of MEMS. In practice that requirement has some error and this utility is designed to estimate the optimum delay of what we think is the time difference of quantum vs MEMS signals to what the actual time delay is.

The two acceleration signals are compared with some variable delay and the minimum difference is estimated.

After loading the join file (*.jlg) and set the range and step for the scan, pressing Delay Scan button will start the data processing.

The bottom chart shows the differences point by point as the cumulative difference for each delay is plotted on the top chart. The minimum of top series is an estimation of the optimum delay we are looking for.
